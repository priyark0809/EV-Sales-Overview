About

Ready to Drive Electric?

Electric car sales neared 14 million in 2023, 95% of which were in China, Europe and the United States

After 2010, electric vehicle (EV) sales saw significant growth, driven by advancements in battery technology, government incentives, and increased consumer awareness about environmental issues..

As governments around the world introduced stricter emissions standards and more substantial subsidies, global EV sales soared. In 2020, despite the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on the global automotive market, EV sales continued to rise, with Europe overtaking China in market share. The emergence of new electric models from established automakers like Ford, Volkswagen, and GM further accelerated EV adoption, while Tesla maintained its leadership..

After 2010, three electric vehicles significantly boosted sales and shaped the EV market. .

  • The Tesla Model S, launched in 2012, redefined electric cars by offering a luxurious, high-performance vehicle with a long range, proving that EVs could rival traditional premium cars.
  • TheNissan Leaf , introduced in 2010, made electric vehicles more accessible as one of the first mass-market EVs. Its affordability and practicality made it a popular choice for urban commuters and showcased the potential of compact electric cars..
  • The Chevrolet Bolt EV, released in 2016, bridged the gap between affordability and range, offering over 200 miles of range at a competitive price. This achievement set a new standard for mid-price EVs and broadened their appeal in the U.S. market. .

The introduction of long-range, high-performance models like the Tesla Model S and mass-market vehicles such as the Nissan Leaf and Chevrolet Bolt EV helped shift public perception, demonstrating that EVs can be practical, desirable, and eco-friendly.
