INCENTIVES FOR EV Updated

The government offers different types of financial incentives to make electric vehicles more affordable for you.

The key mechanisms for getting incentives are:

  • Purchase Incentives: Direct discount provided to the user on the cost of the electric vehicle
  • Intrest Subventions: Subvention on the interest paid on the loan taken for purchasing the electric vehicle
  • Scrapping Incentives: Incentives provided to the scrap dealer for scrapping the old vehicle and decommissioning the battery and recycling the materials
  • Accelerated Depreciation: 150% additional depreciation under the Income Tax Act, 1961

FAME II Scheme Updated

Faster Adoption and Manufacturing of Electric Vehicles in India Phase II (FAME II)

Subsidy Amount: Up to ₹15,000 per kWh of battery capacity
  • Maximum subsidy cap for electric two-wheelers: ₹40,000
  • Maximum subsidy cap for electric cars: ₹1,50,000
  • Valid until: March 31, 2024

Karnataka Subsidy for electric vehicles in Karnataka Besides the central government’s FAME subsidy, Karnataka does not offer any direct incentives to electric vehicle buyers. But that doesn’t make Karnataka less significant than other states in this list. That’s because Karnataka’s EV policy offers subsidies and concessions to EV makers (components, battery, charging enterprise). Recently Tesla announced that it would set up a manufacturing plant in Karnataka. It reflects the contribution of Karnataka to India’s push for electric mobility. Apart from that, Karnataka has also waived road tax and registration charges for battery-powered vehicles.

PLI Scheme

Production Linked Incentive Scheme for Automobile and Auto Components

Total Outlay: ₹25,938 crore
  • Incentives for manufacturers of EVs and components
  • Focus on Advanced Automotive Technology products

How subsidy scheme boost EV sales in India

The second primary reason for the hesitancy of EV adoption is the lack of charging infrastructure. It has triggered range anxiety among the buyers

  • The FAME scheme has been instrumental in boosting EV sales in India.
  • It has provided a much-needed push to the EV market, making electric vehicles more affordable and accessible to consumers.
  • The scheme has also encouraged manufacturers to invest in the production of EVs and their components, leading to increased competition and innovation in the EV market.
  • With attractive central and state government subsidies, the buyers can consider EVs as a feasible alternative to conventional vehicles.
  • Apart from private vehicles, the FAME scheme also supports commercial vehicles. Hence, expect state governments to procure more battery-powered vehicles for public transport.
