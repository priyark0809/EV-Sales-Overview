Evolution of Electric Vehicles

From 1830s to Present Day

1830-1880

The Early Pioneers

First electric vehicles emerged with inventors like Robert Anderson, Anyos Jedlik, and Thomas Davenport creating experimental prototypes. Gaston Plante invented the first rechargeable lead-acid battery in 1860s.

1880-1914

The Golden Age

Electric vehicles gained popularity, comprising 38% of vehicles on American roads. William Morrison built the first practical electric car in the US, capable of carrying 12 people at 20 mph.

The First Hybrid Car


The first hybrid car, built by engineer Ferdinand Porsche in 1899, was the System Lohner-Porsche Mixte, which used a gas engine to supply power to an electric motor that drove the carâ€™s front wheels.
1914-1970

The Decline

Mass production of Ford's Model T and cheap gasoline led to the dominance of internal combustion engines. Electric vehicles almost disappeared from the market by the 1930s.

1970-2003

The Revival

Oil crisis sparked renewed interest in electric vehicles. Toyota Prius launched in 1997 as the first mass-produced hybrid vehicle, marking a significant turning point.

2003-2020

The Revolution

Tesla Motors formed in 2003, revolutionizing electric vehicles. Nissan LEAF launched in 2010. Battery technology improved significantly, making EVs more practical and affordable.

2021-Present

The Tipping Point

Over 26 million EVs on roads globally. Major automakers commit to electric future. Norway leads with 80% new car sales being electric. Global EV sales exceeded 10 million in 2022.
